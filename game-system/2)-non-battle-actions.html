2) Non-Battle Actions

(Credit to Bee for the original system from which this was modified!)

Success and failure of non-battle actions (regular actions) depend on the difficulty of the action as determined by the GM. The difficulty may be determined based on a number of factors, including but not limited to the character's condition, skills, the environmental conditions and situation at hand.

The roll of the dice in this case is taken as a 'lower is better' system, where rolling a 1 is the best result. Note: A 1 does not mean a total success, just the best possible outcome given the situation. The reverse is also true - rolling the highest number on the dice is not necessarily the worst outcome. It is possible to roll the absolute higher number on the dice, and still succeed in carrying out your action. However, this means that you may have a complication introduced - such as the success not going entirely your way, or taking much longer than you expected, causing a situation to arise.

The dice used in this system as as follows:

d1 d2 d4 d6 d8 d10 d12 d20 d30 d100

Actions that have some small amount of chance, but may be on the simpler side, could roll a d4. More difficult actions may have larger dice, such as a d12 or even a d20.

Tasks that are trivially simple and easy are considered to be automatic successes, or assumed as a d1. Tasks that are truly down to pure chance of passing or failing are assumed as a d2.

A d2 can be substituted for a coin, where Heads is 1 and Tails is 2.

Example 1:

Ness wants to steal a fish from a small cat. He rolls a d4 and gets a 1. Ness neatly snatches the fish from the cat.

Example 2:

Ness wants to do a triple flip off the back of a skateboard going 90mph down the highway and land in passenger seat of a convertible driving behind him. Ness rolls a d30 to attempt this and gets an 18. Ness manages to kinda hop off the skateboard, but its not even a flip, and he gets run over by the convertible.

Utilising Traits

Traits are general statements and guidelines that are attributes of your character derived from their background, their species, magic or an item. These traits can give a certain edge in certain situations.

The GM may decide to drop your dice by one level if it is somewhat related to your trait, or by two levels if it is absolutely related to your trait. This is a bit of a discretion based system - feel free to discuss if you feel like the numbers need to be shifted around.

Example:

Let's say you are in a cooking competition. You are a regular person who maybe doesn't know how to cook all that much, and you're up against a professional chef. The professional chef has the trait Chef.

The first round is to make a cheese sandwich. It's a fairly easy task, but a cheese sandwich has potential to be elevated. You can roll a d4 - rolling a 4 will just make a regular sandwich, but a 1 will make it taste really good, such as by toasting it to just the right amount, adding just enough cheese, etc. For a professional chef, this kind of work is extremely routine - the chef will roll a d1 (which is an automatic 1), which means that the chef's sandwich will taste much better.
