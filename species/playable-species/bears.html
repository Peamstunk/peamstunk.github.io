The World
Bears

Bears are the primary denizens of The High Bear Nation. They are, as the name suggests, much like real life bears, although fully capable of walking on their hind legs and having a civilisation.

Description and Varieties

Bears are, much like their real-life equivalents, large mammalian creatures, often very friend shaped and yet also having a reputation for being ferocious and dangerous. However, these bears typically stand on their hind-legs and are much better at walking as such, though being on all fours is not an uncommon sight. The overwhelming majority of bears are found in and around The High Bear Nation, though specific locales may have more of one variety than another.

Below is a list of some of bear varieties:

  • Black Bears are the most common bear and are known for their arboreal lifestyle. They are found all across the High Bear Nation.
  • Brown Bears are the bears with the largest variation in sizes, though they are often the most famously known of the bears.
  • Polar Bears are the largest and strongest of all bears, and are typically found exclusively around the snow-capped peaks of the mountains.
  • Pandas are a unique kind of bear with a distinct black and white fur pattern, and typically found in the bamboo forests bordering the mountain range.
  • Sun Bears are the smallest of the bear varieties, and are typically found in the jungles at the foothills of the southern mountains.

Society

Bear society is largely based around The High Bear Nation, dwelling in the large mountain ranges and the areas around it. Most bears live in smaller tribes and villages scattered across the highlands, typically autonomous villages, but often congregate in the nation's central valleys and mountain caverns for festivities.

Honey is considered a valuable commodity among bears. Being an apiarist is a respected line of work, and brings with it much prestige. Honey is used as a universal material, as currency, as food, as medicine, as drink (via mead), and more.

Bears tend to wear clothes that are inspired by Scottish highlanders, Tibetans and Incas, depending on the region they hail from. Often, it is a mix of two or all three. It is almost unheard of to see a bear without clothing of some sort - public nudity is not acceptable among bears.

Due to the mountainous and forest-filled nature of their homeland, most bears are skilled climbers. Climbing is considered both a means of getting around as well as a recreational pastime activity.
